## 3. Design & UX decisions

- **Dark glass navigation + footer.** The real Kensons logo (`/logo.png`) is a gold car silhouette
  with a **white** wordmark — designed for dark backgrounds. So the floating pill nav and the footer
  use a near-black glass treatment that shows the real logo perfectly, and the light-background
  **emblem** (`/images/emblem.png`) is used as the favicon. This honours the genuine brand asset
  rather than recolouring it.
- **Palette confirmed by the logo (logo authority rule).** The logo uses **gold** (the car) and
  **red** ("Auto Repair · Collision Centre"). Final palette: carbon `#1A1A1A`/`#0F0F0F`, brand red
  `#C8102E`, champagne gold `#C9A961`. Light mood with dramatic dark sections.
- **Review stars are champagne gold**, not red — the conventional premium rating colour, and it ties
  to the "This is Art" gold accent. (Brief suggested "primary brand colour"; gold reads better here.)
- **Service detail pages** are generated by **one dynamic route** (`src/pages/services/[slug].astro`)
  that statically builds all 6 URLs (`/services/accident-repair`, `/resprays`, `/insurance-claims`,
  `/servicing`, `/brakes-wheels`, `/customisation`). Output is identical to 6 separate files — just
  DRY and guaranteed consistent.
- **Contact form = "Get a Photo Estimate".** This is a static site (no server/backend, per the
  Cloudflare Pages target), so the form composes a pre-filled **email** (mailto) and the primary
  photo channel is the **WhatsApp deep-link** with a pre-filled "send photos of my car" message —
  which mirrors Kensons' actual workflow. The original Elementor form's file-upload field cannot be
  reproduced statically without a backend; WhatsApp covers it cleanly. No data is stored on a server.
